Hello!

I'm a researcher at OpenAI, where I lead the Post-training Frontiers team. We train the agentic models shipped across Codex, the API, and ChatGPT Thinking/Pro (e.g., o3, GPT-5 Thinking, GPT-5.3 Codex, GPT-5.5, etc.).
My goal is to train and deploy models that provide maximal value to users.

Previously, I was a PhD student at Stanford University, co-advised by [Percy Liang](https://cs.stanford.edu/~pliang/) and [Tatsu Hashimoto](https://thashim.github.io/) and funded by a [Knight-Hennessy Scholarship](https://knight-hennessy.stanford.edu/people/yann-dubois). My research focused on instruction following ([Alpaca](https://github.com/tatsu-lab/stanford_alpaca), [Alpaca-eval](https://github.com/tatsu-lab/alpaca_eval)).
Before that, I traveled around doing research in representation learning and worked at startups including [Grab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grab_(company)), where I built NLP systems for under-researched languages (Thai, Khmer, Burmese, ...) before LLMs were a thing.

Note: please do not contact me about quant jobs; I'm not interested regardless of compensation.
